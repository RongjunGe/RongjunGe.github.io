Biography

Dr. Rongjun Ge is currently an Associate Professor with the School of Instrument Science and Engineering, Southeast University. He received his Ph.D. degree from Southeast University under the supervision of Prof. Yang Chen. During his doctoral study, he was a visiting researcher at Western University, working with Prof. Shuo Li. He subsequently conducted postdoctoral research at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) with Prof. Ge Wang.

His research interests include medical image analysis, low-dose CT reconstruction and enhancement, foundation models for medical imaging, intelligent surgical systems, and human-centered artificial intelligence. His recent work focuses on CT enhancement, foundation model, multimodal representation learning, gaze-guided medical image understanding, and intelligent surgical decision support systems.

He has published more than 40 peer-reviewed papers in leading venues including Nature Communications, IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, Medical Image Analysis, IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology, IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, Neural Networks, CVPR, and MICCAI. His publications have received more than 1,400 citations with an H-index of 21 according to Google Scholar.
